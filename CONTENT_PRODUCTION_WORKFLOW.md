# Content Production Workflow: Grand Blog Tarkin

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** February 3, 2026
**Purpose:** Standardized process for creating high-quality Poli-Sci-Fi content

---

## Overview

This workflow enables consistent production of 3-4 articles per month (7-10 hours per article) using AI-assisted drafting combined with human expertise. The goal is to maintain high quality while achieving sustainable output.

## Workflow Phases

### Phase 1: Research & Planning (2 hours)

#### 1.1 Topic Selection
- Review content calendar and strategy document
- Check trending topics in sci-fi and geopolitics
- Identify gaps in existing content
- Verify topic aligns with one of three pillars:
  - Geopolitics & Sci-Fi
  - Military Strategy & Sci-Fi Tech
  - Societal Impact & Sci-Fi Futures

#### 1.2 Keyword Research
**Tools:** Google Trends, Ahrefs (optional), Google Search Console

**Process:**
1. Identify primary keyword (e.g., "Dune geopolitics")
2. Find 3-5 related long-tail keywords
3. Check search volume and competition
4. Analyze top 10 results for primary keyword
5. Identify content gaps and opportunities

**Deliverable:** Keyword list with search volumes and difficulty scores

#### 1.3 Competitor Analysis
**Review top 5 articles for primary keyword:**
- What do they cover well?
- What do they miss?
- What unique angle can we provide?
- What internal links can we add that they don't have?

**Deliverable:** Competitive analysis notes

#### 1.4 Source Material Review
**For sci-fi content:**
- Re-watch/re-read relevant scenes/chapters
- Review fan wikis for accuracy
- Check academic papers on the work (if available)
- Note specific quotes and examples to use

**For geopolitics content:**
- Review recent news and analysis
- Check academic sources (JSTOR, Google Scholar)
- Identify real-world parallels
- Gather statistics and data points

**Deliverable:** Source notes with citations

#### 1.5 Outline Creation
**Structure:**
```
# Title (60 chars, includes primary keyword)

## Introduction (200-300 words)
- Hook: Compelling opening
- Context: Why this matters
- Thesis: Main argument

## Section 1: [H2 with keyword variation]
### Subsection 1.1 [H3]
### Subsection 1.2 [H3]

## Section 2: [H2 with keyword variation]
### Subsection 2.1 [H3]
### Subsection 2.2 [H3]

## Section 3: [H2 with keyword variation]
### Subsection 3.1 [H3]
### Subsection 3.2 [H3]

## Conclusion (200-300 words)
- Summary of key points
- Broader implications
- Call to action or reflection

---
*Internal links section*
```

**Deliverable:** Detailed outline with H2/H3 structure

#### 1.6 Internal Linking Strategy
**Identify 3-5 existing articles to link to:**
- Related by topic
- Related by theme
- Related by keyword
- Hub articles (Tarkin Doctrine, Sci-Fi Sandbox, etc.)

**Deliverable:** List of internal links with anchor text

### Phase 2: AI-Assisted Drafting (1 hour)

#### 2.1 Prepare AI Prompt
**Template:**
```
I need a 2500-word article for Grand Blog Tarkin, a blog analyzing geopolitics through science fiction.

TOPIC: [Title]

TARGET AUDIENCE:
- Educated readers interested in geopolitics, military strategy, and sci-fi
- Age 25-45, mostly male, tech-savvy
- Familiar with major sci-fi works but want deeper analysis

TONE:
- Analytical but accessible
- Authoritative without being academic
- Engaging storytelling mixed with strategic analysis
- Occasional dry humor

STRUCTURE:
[Paste outline]

REQUIREMENTS:
- Use specific examples from [sci-fi work]
- Connect to real-world geopolitical events
- Include at least one table or comparison
- Write in active voice
- Avoid clich√©s and overused phrases
- Target keyword: [primary keyword]
- Secondary keywords: [list]

INTERNAL LINKS TO INCLUDE:
[List with context]

Please generate the article following this structure.
```

#### 2.2 Generate Initial Draft
**Tools:** Claude (Sonnet 4.5), GPT-4, or Gemini

**Process:**
1. Submit prompt to AI
2. Review output for structure and completeness
3. If needed, request specific sections to be expanded
4. Generate 2-3 variations of introduction and conclusion
5. Select best version or combine elements

**Deliverable:** Raw AI-generated draft (2000-2500 words)

#### 2.3 Generate Variations
**For key sections, request alternatives:**
- "Provide 3 different ways to explain [concept]"
- "Give me 5 real-world examples of [theme]"
- "Suggest 3 alternative conclusions"

**Deliverable:** Alternative sections for consideration

---

### Phase 3: Human Editing & Enhancement (2-3 hours)

#### 3.1 Fact-Checking (30 minutes)
**Verify:**
- Sci-fi plot details and quotes
- Historical events and dates
- Statistics and data points
- Technical terminology
- Author names and work titles

**Tools:**
- Sci-fi wikis (Dune Wiki, Wookieepedia, etc.)
- Wikipedia for basic facts
- Google Scholar for academic claims
- Original source material

**Deliverable:** Fact-checked draft with corrections

#### 3.2 Voice & Style Editing (45 minutes)
**Enhance:**
- Remove AI-isms ("delve," "tapestry," "landscape," "it's important to note")
- Add personal insights and opinions
- Strengthen transitions between sections
- Vary sentence structure and length
- Add specific, vivid examples
- Insert pull quotes or emphasized text
- Ensure consistent tone throughout

**Blog voice characteristics:**
- Confident but not arrogant
- Analytical but not dry
- Engaging but not sensational
- Informed but not pedantic

**Deliverable:** Voice-edited draft

#### 3.3 Strategic Enhancement (30 minutes)
**Add:**
- Unique insights not in competitor articles
- Personal analysis and interpretation
- Connections between multiple sci-fi works
- Predictions or implications for the future
- Controversial or thought-provoking takes (when appropriate)

**Deliverable:** Enhanced draft with unique value

#### 3.4 Internal Linking (15 minutes)
**Insert links:**
- 3-5 internal links to related articles
- Natural anchor text (not "click here")
- Links in context, not forced
- Update linked articles to link back (if needed)

**Deliverable:** Draft with internal links

#### 3.5 Final Polish (30 minutes)
**Review:**
- Grammar and spelling (Grammarly or manual)
- Readability (Hemingway App or manual)
- Flow and coherence
- Paragraph length (vary between short and long)
- Subheading clarity
- Introduction hook strength
- Conclusion impact

**Deliverable:** Polished draft ready for SEO optimization

### Phase 4: SEO Optimization (30 minutes)

#### 4.1 Meta Data
**Create:**
- **Title tag:** 50-60 characters, includes primary keyword
- **Meta description:** 150-160 characters, compelling, includes keyword
- **Slug:** Short, keyword-rich, hyphenated (e.g., "dune-geopolitics-arrakis")

**Example:**
```yaml
title: "The Geopolitics of Arrakis: Resource Control in Dune"
description: "How Frank Herbert's Dune mirrors real-world resource wars, imperial competition, and indigenous resistance. In-depth geopolitical analysis."
slug: dune-geopolitics-arrakis
```

#### 4.2 Header Optimization
**Verify:**
- One H1 (title)
- 3-5 H2s with keyword variations
- 2-3 H3s under each H2
- Headers are descriptive and scannable
- Natural keyword inclusion (not stuffed)

#### 4.3 Keyword Optimization
**Check:**
- Primary keyword in: title, first paragraph, at least one H2, conclusion
- Keyword density: 1-2% (natural, not forced)
- LSI keywords (related terms) throughout
- Avoid keyword stuffing

#### 4.4 Image Optimization
**Prepare:**
- Alt text with descriptive keywords
- File names with keywords (e.g., "dune-arrakis-power-structure.webp")
- Compressed for web (WebP format preferred)
- Appropriate dimensions

#### 4.5 Schema Markup
**Verify frontmatter includes:**
```yaml
---
layout: ../../layouts/PostLayout.astro
title: "Article Title"
description: "Meta description"
author: aelkus
slug: article-slug
pubDate: 2026-02-03T00:00:00Z
keywords:
  - primary keyword
  - secondary keyword
  - related keyword
tags:
  - category1
  - category2
---
```

**Deliverable:** SEO-optimized article ready for visual content

### Phase 5: Visual Content Creation (1-2 hours)

#### 5.1 Diagram/Infographic Creation
**Tools:**
- Excalidraw (hand-drawn style, free)
- Mermaid.js (code-based diagrams, embedded)
- Figma (polished graphics, free tier)
- Canva (templates, free tier)

**Process:**
1. Identify key concept that needs visualization
2. Sketch rough layout
3. Create digital version
4. Export as WebP (with PNG fallback)
5. Add to article with descriptive caption

**Examples:**
- Power structure diagrams (Arrakis, CHOAM)
- Decision trees (Dark Forest Theory)
- Comparison tables (Cyberpunk vs. Solarpunk)
- Timelines (Deterrence evolution)
- Process flows (Swarm coordination)

**Deliverable:** 1-2 custom diagrams per article

#### 5.2 Featured Image
**Options:**
1. **AI-generated:** Midjourney, DALL-E (concept art style)
2. **Public domain:** Wikimedia Commons, NASA images
3. **Creative Commons:** Flickr, Unsplash (with attribution)
4. **Book covers:** Public domain or fair use

**Requirements:**
- 1200x630px minimum (for social sharing)
- WebP format with PNG fallback
- Relevant to article topic
- Visually striking

**Deliverable:** Featured image optimized for web

#### 5.3 Image Integration
**Add to article:**
- Featured image at top (in frontmatter)
- Diagrams/infographics in relevant sections
- Alt text for all images
- Captions where appropriate
- Credits/attribution if needed

**Deliverable:** Article with visual content integrated

### Phase 6: Publishing & Promotion (1 hour)

#### 6.1 Pre-Publication Checklist
- [ ] Final proofread (read aloud)
- [ ] All links work (internal and external)
- [ ] Images load correctly
- [ ] Mobile preview looks good
- [ ] Meta data is complete
- [ ] Author attribution is correct
- [ ] Date is correct
- [ ] Tags and keywords are set

#### 6.2 Publication
**Process:**
1. Commit article to Git repository
2. Push to GitHub
3. Cloudflare Pages auto-deploys
4. Verify live article looks correct
5. Test on mobile device
6. Check page speed (PageSpeed Insights)

**Deliverable:** Live article on blogtarkin.com

#### 6.3 Update Related Content
**Tasks:**
1. Add new article to homepage (index.astro)
2. Update related articles to link to new article
3. Update author page if needed
4. Regenerate sitemap (automatic with Astro)

**Deliverable:** Site updated with new content

#### 6.4 Social Media Promotion
**Twitter/X:**
- Thread format (5-7 tweets)
- Key insights from article
- Link to full article
- Relevant hashtags (#scifi #geopolitics #Dune)
- Tag relevant accounts (authors, publications)

**LinkedIn:**
- Professional angle
- 2-3 paragraph summary
- Link to article
- Tag thought leaders in comments

**Reddit:**
- Share in relevant subreddits (r/scifi, r/geopolitics, r/Dune)
- Participate in discussion (don't just drop link)
- Follow 10:1 rule (10 comments per 1 post)

**Hacker News:**
- Submit if technical/strategic angle
- Engage with comments
- Don't be defensive

**Deliverable:** Social media posts published

#### 6.5 Newsletter
**If weekly digest:**
- Add article summary to next newsletter
- Include pull quote or key insight
- Link to full article
- Mention in "This Week in Poli-Sci-Fi" section

**If standalone:**
- Send dedicated email to subscribers
- Compelling subject line
- Article summary + link
- Call to action (comment, share)

**Deliverable:** Newsletter sent (if applicable)

#### 6.6 Analytics Setup
**Track:**
- Google Search Console: Index status, search queries
- Cloudflare Analytics: Traffic, referrers, geography
- Social media: Engagement, clicks, shares

**Set reminder:** Check analytics in 1 week, 1 month, 3 months

**Deliverable:** Analytics tracking configured

## Quality Standards

### Content Quality
- **Accuracy:** All facts verified, sources cited
- **Originality:** Unique insights, not just summary
- **Depth:** Thorough analysis, not surface-level
- **Readability:** Clear, engaging, well-structured
- **Voice:** Consistent with blog tone

### SEO Quality
- **Keywords:** Natural integration, 1-2% density
- **Structure:** Proper header hierarchy, scannable
- **Links:** 3-5 internal links, relevant external links
- **Meta:** Compelling title and description
- **Performance:** Fast load time, mobile-friendly

### Visual Quality
- **Relevance:** Images support content
- **Clarity:** Diagrams are easy to understand
- **Optimization:** Fast loading, proper format
- **Accessibility:** Alt text for all images

## Batch Production Tips

### Weekly Batch (Recommended)
**Monday:** Research & outline 2 articles (4 hours)
**Tuesday:** AI drafting for both articles (2 hours)
**Wednesday:** Edit article 1 (3 hours)
**Thursday:** Edit article 2 (3 hours)
**Friday:** SEO, visuals, publish article 1 (3 hours)
**Weekend:** Visuals, publish article 2, promotion (3 hours)

**Total:** 18 hours/week = 2 articles/week (unsustainable)

### Sustainable Pace
**Week 1:** Research, draft, edit article 1 (6 hours)
**Week 2:** Visuals, publish, promote article 1 (3 hours) + Research, draft article 2 (3 hours)
**Week 3:** Edit, visuals, publish article 2 (6 hours)
**Week 4:** Promotion, analytics review, planning (3 hours)

**Total:** 18 hours/month = 2 articles/month (sustainable)

## Troubleshooting

### Problem: AI draft is too generic
**Solution:**
- Provide more specific examples in prompt
- Request multiple variations
- Add personal insights during editing
- Use AI for structure, write key sections yourself

### Problem: Article is too long
**Solution:**
- Split into two articles (Part 1 & 2)
- Move tangential content to separate article
- Tighten prose, remove redundancy
- Focus on core argument

### Problem: Can't find good visuals
**Solution:**
- Create simple diagram in Excalidraw
- Use text-based visualization (ASCII art, tables)
- Commission custom art (Fiverr, $20-50)
- Use public domain book covers

### Problem: Low engagement after publishing
**Solution:**
- Share in more communities
- Engage with comments actively
- Update article with reader feedback
- Create follow-up content
- Improve headline and meta description
